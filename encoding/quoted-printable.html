About Quoted-Printable

Quoted-Printable is a binary-to-text encoding scheme that represents 8-bit data using printable ASCII characters. It's defined in RFC 2045 and is commonly used in email messages.

Encoding Rules:
  • Non-printable characters are encoded as = followed by two hexadecimal digits
  • Lines longer than 76 characters are soft-wrapped with = at the end
  • Spaces and tabs at the end of lines are encoded
  • Used primarily in email headers and message bodies
