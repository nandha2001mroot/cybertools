About UUencoding

UUencoding (Unix-to-Unix encoding) is a binary-to-text encoding scheme that was used to send binary files over email and Usenet. It converts 8-bit binary data into 6-bit ASCII characters.

Historical Context:
  • Widely used in the 1980s and 1990s
  • Used for sending binary files over email
  • Part of Usenet newsgroup culture
  • Now largely replaced by MIME
