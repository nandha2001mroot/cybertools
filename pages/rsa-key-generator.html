About RSA

RSA (Rivest–Shamir–Adleman) is one of the first public-key cryptosystems and is widely used for secure data transmission. It's commonly used for:

  • Digital signatures
  • Secure key exchange
  • Email encryption
  • SSL/TLS certificates
  • Software distribution
Security Note: RSA keys should be at least 2048 bits for security. Keys smaller than 1024 bits are considered insecure.
