About Phishing

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, dupes a victim into opening an email, instant message, or text message.

Common Phishing Indicators:
  • Spoofed Sender: Email appears from trusted source but isn't
  • Urgent Language: Creates sense of urgency to act immediately
  • Suspicious Links: URLs that don't match the claimed destination
  • Poor Grammar: Spelling and grammar mistakes in professional emails
  • Unexpected Attachments: Files you weren't expecting to receive
  • Requests for Information: Asks for sensitive data like passwords
